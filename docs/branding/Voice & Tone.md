# Branding — Voice & Tone

## Purpose

How Aura sounds in product UI and marketing, derived from the copy that exists today (landing page, metadata, in-product strings).

## Current State

The voice is **confident, plain-spoken, and outcome-focused**, aimed at institutional decision-makers (chairmen, principals, IQAC, administrators) and everyday users (faculty, students, parents).

## Implementation

### Principles (observed in the landing + product copy)
1. **Outcome over feature.** Lead with the result: "Build an entire semester timetable in minutes," "Stop managing chaos. Start running your institution." Frame value as pain → outcome.
2. **Plain and direct.** Short sentences, concrete claims, minimal jargon. Numbers are honest (dashboards show real seeded counts).
3. **Confident, not hypey.** Strong but credible ("accreditation-ready from day one"), avoid superlatives that can't be backed by the product.
4. **Respectful of the domain.** Uses correct Indian higher-ed vocabulary (NAAC, NIRF, NBA, AISHE, IQAC, AQAR, CO/PO, CIA) accurately.
5. **Reassuring on trust.** Security/compliance stated calmly ("Row-Level Security," "DPDP Act 2023," "hosted in India").
6. **Helpful in-app.** Error/empty states are clear and actionable (e.g. AI "not configured" message, offline-scheduler banner) rather than technical or blaming.

### Mechanics
- **Sentence case** for most UI; **UPPERCASE eyebrows** for section labels.
- Indian English; INR (₹) for money.
- Address the reader as "you" / "your institution."
- Prefer verbs ("Manage," "Track," "Publish") over nouns.

### Do / Don't
| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Conflict-free timetables in one click." | "Revolutionary AI-powered synergy." |
| "Built-in NAAC/NIRF exports." | Vague "world-class" claims |
| State real numbers | Inflate metrics |
| Calm trust statements | Fear-based selling |
